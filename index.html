Classical 2-flavour QED2

Classical real-time simulation of the bosonized 1+1D 2-flavour QED with closed boundary condition. One may interpret flavour 1 as proton and flavour 2 as electron. The quench is used to test whether a vacuum disturbance can nucleate hydrogen-like objects in a 1+1D universe.

The displayed fields are φc = (φ1 + φ2)/√2 and φs = (φ1 - φ2)/√2, namely the symmetric and antisymmetric combinations of the two flavour fields. Positive and negative hydrogen are represented by kink and antikink configurations of φs. The default quench is a local Gaussian momentum kick in flavour 1, which in the (φc,φs) basis is implemented by ∂tφc(x,0)=∂tφs(x,0)=A exp[-(x/σ)^2].
