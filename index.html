Classical 2-flavour QED2

Classical real-time simulation of the bosonized 1+1D 2-flavour QED with closed boundary condition. One may interpret flavour 1 as proton and flavour 2 as electron. The quench is used to test whether a vacuum disturbance can nucleate hydrogen-like objects in a 1+1D universe.

The displayed fields are \(\phi_c=(\phi_1+\phi_2)/\sqrt{2}\) and \(\phi_s=(\phi_1-\phi_2)/\sqrt{2}\), namely the symmetric and antisymmetric combinations of the two flavour fields. Positive and negative hydrogen are represented by kink and antikink configurations of \(\phi_s\). The default quench is a local Gaussian momentum kick in flavour 1, which in the \((\phi_c,\phi_s)\) basis is implemented by \(\partial_t\phi_c(x,0)=\partial_t\phi_s(x,0)=A e^{-(x/\sigma)^2}\).

Dashed lines mark topological sectors \(n\sqrt{2\pi}\).
